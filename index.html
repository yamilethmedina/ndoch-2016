Types of Affordable Housing

  • Privately owned subsidized housing - Less than 80% AMI (Area Median Income) adjusted for family size - Apply at individual development
  • Public Housing - Less than 80% AMI adjusted for family size - Apply with Miami-Dade
  • Emergency Shelter Grant (ESG) - Temporary rehousing assistance for people at or below 30% AMI
  • Section 8 - Less than 80% AMI adjusted for family size - Apply with Miami-Dade
  • City of Miami’s First-Time Homeowner Program - 0% percent deferred loans to first-time homebuyers purchasing a property in the City of Miami. Eligibility for the program is based on a household income less than or equal to 80% AMI and adjusted for family size
  • City of Miami’s Homeowner Programs (Single-Family Rehabilitation, Single-Family Emergency Rehabilitation, and Single-Family Replacement) - less than or equal to 80% AMI, adjusted for family size.
  • Housing for Persons with AIDS (HOPWA) Program
  • Neighborhood Stabilization Plan (NSP)

Income Requirements

  • Section 8: Extremely-Low to Very-Low (up to 60% of Average Median Income)
  • Workforce Housing: Very-Low - Low (60% - 80% of Average )

Application Process for Workforce Housing

  1. Visit Property
  2. Ask for Availability (If not, put on waiting list)
  3. Application Fee $35-$100
  4. Documents: Check Stubs, Bank Statement, W2, Birth Certificate, Social Security
  5. Credit Card and Criminal Check
  6. If Approved, Must Pay Holding Fee of $200-$300 (Refundable)
* This process must be done for each property
* 2-week turnaround

Required Documents

To apply for Section 8 or public housing, applicants must provide the following. (To apply at a privately owned public housing development more documents MAY be required): Required documents (From http://www.miamidade.gov/housing/apply-public-housing.asp#3)

  • Picture ID, such as a driver’s license
  • Medicare and/or Medicaid cards
  • Social security cards for all persons appearing on the application
  • Proof of age and other documents related to family or household make-up including birth certificates, divorce and custody papers, citizenship, immigration papers, etc.
  • Proof of all income, including letter from Social Security, Veteran’s Administration, pensions, employment, and child support payments. The verification of Social Security benefits must be requested prior to the applicant’s scheduled appointment.
  • Proof of assets such as bank accounts, life insurance policies, etc.
  • Your doctor’s name, address, and telephone number
  • Names, addresses, and phone numbers of at least two people to contact in case of emergency
  • Your last three landlords’ names, addresses, and telephone numbers and copies of your last three utility bills
  • Verification of child care expenses
  • Verification of full-time student status for dependents over 17 years of age and record of attendance for all school-age children
  • If the head of household or spouse is over 62 years old or over, or a person with disabilities, you may bring verification of medical expenses or anticipated expenses such as, health insurance payments, on-going prescriptions, doctor’s visits, payback agreements on hospital bills, etc.

Barriers to Affordable Housing

When applying for affordable housing at private developments, renters must:

  • Apply in person at each development
  • Pay excessive fees and deposits (in excess of $1,000)
  • Pass credit and background checks with excessively high barriers
  • Supply burdensome documentation and financial projections
  • Wait weeks for approval

Benefits

Why would people support A.H.?
It's important for Miami to decrease the brain drain. Affordable housing could help to attract and retain top talent for the workforce. The affordability and accessibility of Miami-Dade County’s housing has widespread economic impacts.

The affordability and accessibility of Miami-Dade County’s housing has widespread economic impacts. There is now a growing recognition of the important link between an adequate supply of workforce housing and sustainable economic growth. In fact, many of Florida’s leading business sectors, including professional services, retail trade, and health care, are finding it increasingly difficult to attract and maintain workers in entry and mid-management positions. There is now increasing evidence that working families have begun to move to locations which have more affordable housing, both in and outside the State of Florida.

Development of affordable housing increases spending and employment in the surrounding economy, acts as an important source of revenue for local governments, and reduces the likelihood of foreclosure and its associated costs.
